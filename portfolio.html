motion.social - Visit Site

In its infancy, motion.social was simply a web page with a collection of bike routes. The Co-Founders knew they wanted to target active people who are motivated by social connection over performance data, but exactly how that translated into a product was undecided.

Then I was brought on as a UX/UI Designer and first hire, and I immediately began conducting user interviews. I sat one-on-one with active people and asked them about their habits, goals, and frustrations. Through this I discovered that a common frustration was planning group activities. Long e-mail threads, inescapable group texts, and generic planning platforms were a pain to use. After examining the market, it was clear that many products focused on the 'during' (tracking) and 'after' (posting) part of an activity, but not the very beginning phase of getting a group of people together. Thus, with these findings, I designed a planning tool, with features more precisely targeted at socially and physically active users.

Cascade Orienteering Club - Visit Site

As an enthusiastic orienteer who loves introducing newcomers to the sport, I took on a complete redesign of the Cascade Orienteering Club web page. I did this as my capstone project for BLOC, an online bootcamp course, in lieu of a much simpler project for an imaginary company. I not only wanted real-world experience, but I wanted my work to mean something.

I began by interviewing users, from prospective participants to long-time veterans. My task was to design a page that would be welcoming to the former, yet efficent for the latter. With this in mind, I designed the front page to have a clear welcome mat for newcomers, which would direct them to friendly newcomer content, while creating one-click opportunities for veterans to register, get directions to the next event, and find the most recent results.

In addition to designing the site, I also wrote the Wordpress theme from scratch (PHP), in order to have complete control over the design.

The club still uses the template today, and the design is under consideration to be replicated for clubs across the country.
